Design a Job Scheduler

Problem overview

Design a distributed job scheduler (think Airflow / Quartz / AWS EventBridge) that triggers and runs jobs reliably at large scale.

  • Throughput: 10,000+ jobs/sec, with sub-2s trigger precision
  • Job types: immediate, future one-shot, recurring (cron), and ad-hoc
  • Reliability: at-least-once execution, retries w/ backoff, timeouts, per-job concurrency limits
  • History: searchable execution history (timestamps, attempts, outcome, logs) for 1 year
The whole problem in one line: separate scheduling from execution, then make each side independently scalable and fault-tolerant.

What I want to hear — by phase

1 · Requirements & scoping (first ~5 min)

  • Restates the functional core: create job (immediate/future/cron), ad-hoc submit, view status + history, configure retry/timeout/concurrency.
  • Pins non-functionals with numbers: 10k/s, <2s jitter, at-least-once, 1-yr retention, HA. Jumping to boxes without an SLA = weaker signal.
  • Back-of-envelope: 10k/s → ~864M runs/day → this drives the hot/cold history decision later.

Q: Dispatch 10k jobs/sec with <2s precision without melting the data store?

Strong answer: Don't poll one hot table with SELECT … WHERE due. Use time-bucketed / sharded partitions indexed on next-run-time, or a tiered design: durable store for the long tail + an in-memory timing wheel / sorted set for the imminent window. Pre-fetch the next minute into memory.

Q: Provide at-least-once execution while preventing duplicate harm?

Strong answer: Accept that crashes ⇒ duplicates. Make handlers idempotent via dedup keys / idempotency tokens; use visibility timeouts + leases so a slow worker can't cause a double-run. Say it plainly: “exactly-once delivery is impractical; I get exactly-once effects via idempotency.”

Q: Manage worker lifecycle, scaling, and safe draining at this throughput?

Strong answer: Heartbeats + leases; reassign jobs from dead workers; autoscale on queue depth; graceful drain on deploy (stop pulling, finish in-flight, ack).

Q: Keep 1 year of history searchable at this volume?

Strong answer: Hot/cold split — recent runs in a fast queryable store (Postgres/Cassandra), older runs aged to cheap cold storage (S3/Parquet) with partition pruning for search.

4 · Red flags that drop the score

▸Polling a single jobs table for due jobs
Hot partition, lock contention, missed precision under spikes. The single biggest tell of inexperience on this problem.
▸Claiming exactly-once without idempotency / dedup
Crashes and retries make duplicates inevitable → double-charges, double-emails, corrupted state.
▸No backpressure / capacity planning
The “thundering herd” at the top of every minute (everyone's 0 * * * * fires at once) → cascading retries, correlated failure. Want to hear jitter/spreading, rate limits, queue-depth autoscaling.

5 · Bonus signals (senior / staff)

  • +Names the top-of-minute burst unprompted and spreads load.
  • +Distinguishes delivery guarantee vs execution guarantee.
  • +Discusses observability: per-run logs, status transitions, alerting on stuck/failed jobs.
  • +Multi-tenant fairness / per-job concurrency caps so one noisy job can't starve others.
