👁 How to use Glimpse

You're reading this inside Glimpse — published from a terminal, rendered live in Chrome. That's the whole idea.

💡 In one line: Glimpse is a shared screen between you and your AI agent. Instead of cramming everything into the terminal, the agent shows you clean, interactive pages — diagrams, tables, demos — in a real browser.

A quick user story

M
Mia just joined a team and needs to understand a service.

She asks her agent: “Walk me through how the thumbnail service works.”

The agent reads the codebase, then — instead of dumping three screens of terminal text — it runs one command. A clean page opens in Mia's browser: a labeled architecture diagram, tabbed components, and collapsible operational notes. She reads it like a doc, clicks through the tabs, and keeps it. When she asks a follow-up, the page updates in place.

(a) What's the story, simply?

You ask for something. The agent does the work and hands you a web page to read, not a wall of terminal text.

(b) Why use it?

A terminal can't draw a diagram, show tabs, or let you collapse sections. So a smart agent is forced to either dump everything (overwhelming) or over-summarize (you lose detail). Neither is how a real colleague would show you their work — they'd open a doc or sketch on a whiteboard. Glimpse gives the agent that whiteboard.

(c) What problem does it solve?

The gap between how much an agent can produce and how little a terminal can show. Glimpse closes it: the agent's best thinking becomes something you can actually read, click, and keep.

Try it (3 steps)

# 1) open the canvas in Chrome
glimpse open

# 2) publish anything (a file, or pipe HTML straight in)
echo '<h1>hi 👋</h1>' | glimpse publish hello "My first artifact"

# 3) watch it appear in the sidebar — no refresh
