Backfill thumbnails without downtime

Goal: every existing image gets a webp thumbnail, with no read downtime and no destructive writes. Read this before I touch code.

Now

Thumbnails are generated on upload only. ~2.1M originals predate that and have none.

Target

100% coverage. Reads fall back to on-the-fly resize until an image is backfilled.

Approach

  1. Add a nullable thumb_key column — an online, non-blocking migration. Nothing reads it yet.
  2. Backfill in batches of 1,000 off a work queue, idempotent by image id, throttled on worker queue depth.
  3. Cut reads over once coverage > 99%, keeping the resize fallback so a miss is never a hard error.
Risk: resize CPU on the workers — throttle batch rate on queue depth so live uploads keep priority.
Open question: keep originals forever, or expire at 1 year? Needs a product call — see the comparison playbook for the options.
