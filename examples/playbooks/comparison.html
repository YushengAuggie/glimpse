Job queue: SQS or a Postgres-backed queue?

Name the decision, show each option's real benefit and cost, and recommend only when the evidence supports one.

Recommended

Amazon SQS

Benefit
Absorbs spikes; effectively zero ops; scales workers on queue depth.
Cost
Another dependency; at-least-once delivery → workers must be idempotent.

Postgres-backed queue

Benefit
One datastore; enqueue is transactional with the job row.
Cost
Polling load on the primary; throughput caps well below SQS.
Why SQS: upload traffic is spiky and the resizer workers are already idempotent, so at-least-once delivery costs us nothing. Flip to Postgres only if sustained volume stays under ~50 jobs/s and we want to drop the dependency.
