Highlight-to-chat

Select any passage on this page and ask the agent about it — the answer threads right next to your highlight.

Try it: drag-select a sentence below (about ten characters or more), then click Ask in the little toolbar that appears, type a question, and click Send (or press ⌘Enter). Your question pins to that passage; the agent's reply lands in the margin.

How the round-trip works

When you ask, the question is captured with a text-quote anchor — the exact words plus a little surrounding context — so the highlight can be re-found even if the document re-renders. Nothing is stored in the page itself: the question is written straight to a per-document thread file, which is the durable source of truth.

A small bridge running alongside your agent notices the new question and hands it over. The agent answers with one command, and the canvas streams that answer back into this page without a reload — no flash, no scroll jump. Because the conversation lives in a file, it survives a refresh, a browser restart, and even a fresh agent session.

Why anchor to a selection

A plain chat box loses track of what you were asking about. Anchoring each thread to the passage it concerns keeps the discussion legible weeks later, and lets several independent threads coexist on one long document without turning into a scroll-back hunt.

This paragraph is a good one to test the multiple-highlights behaviour: highlight a phrase here, ask something, then highlight a different phrase above and ask again. Each gets its own numbered marker and its own thread.
